Oil and Gas Law

Our firm offers experienced representation in all aspects of oil and gas transactions and litigation. We represent publicly traded corporations, large and small independent producers, individuals and trusts in sophisticated oil and gas law transactions and disputes.

Our oil and gas transactional practice is led by Pat Murphy, one of the most experienced oil and gas attorneys in the Fort Worth area. Mr. Murphy is board certified in oil, gas and mineral law by the Texas Board of Legal Specialization and is a frequent speaker on oil and gas law topics at industry and legal seminars. Murphy Mahon Keffler & Farrier is a full-service law firm for the oil and gas industry. We represent our clients in numerous types of energy transactions, including:

  • The purchase and sale of oil and gas properties
  • Farm-out and exploration agreements
  • Operating Issues
  • Oil and gas title matters
  • Asset-based production loans
  • Oil and gas loan workouts and restructuring agreements

In addition to these oil and gas transactions, we also offer our oil and gas clients a complete range of business law services, from business formation advice through mergers and acquisitions to licensing and distribution agreements.

Experienced Representation in Oil and Gas Law Disputes and Litigation

Murphy Mahon Keffler & Farrier also handles disputes and litigation that arise during all phases of oil and gas exploration, development and production. We have substantial experience in lease termination cases, royalty disputes, mineral title disputes and operating agreement disputes.

Commercial Transactions

At Murphy Mahon Keffler & Farrier, we handle commercial transactions on a daily basis for a wide range of clients. Along with commercial litigation, commercial transactions are the backbone of our legal practice.

Our law firm handles commercial transactions for clients in a wide variety of industries:

  • Oil, gas and mineral industries: We negotiate the acquisition, lease and sale of oil and gas assets, both unimproved and developed. For more information, also see the Oil and Gas Law page of this Web site.
  • Commercial real estate: We handle all legal aspects of the purchase, sale and development of land for commercial purposes. For more information, also see the Commercial Real Estate page of this Web site.
  • Purchase and sale of ongoing businesses: We handle the transfer of stocks or assets in mergers and acquisitions of ongoing businesses. More information is available on the Mergers and Acquisitions page of this Web site.
  • Construction transactions: We negotiate construction agreements for the improvement of real property on behalf of contractors, architects, owners or general contractors.

Mergers and Acquisitions

At Murphy Mahon Keffler & Farrier, we are called upon frequently to negotiate complex mergers and acquisitions for our clients, both in the oil and gas industry and in other fields. Our attorneys have extensive experience handling all legal aspects of mergers and acquisitions of privately held corporations.

Buyers and sellers often have differing concerns. We regularly handle both sides of mergers and acquisitions and are deeply familiar with how best to protect the interests of our client, whether our client is the buyer or seller.

Representing the Buyer in Mergers and Acquisitions

When our client is the buyer in a merger or acquisition, the primary concerns involve the financial integrity of the business and the ease with which the business will be able to continue operations. To protect the buyer's investment, our law firm provides legal guidance on these and other matters:

  • Transfer of employees
  • Obtaining financing via asset-based lending
  • Drafting employment contracts with new employees
  • Restructuring the business entity
  • Limitation of liability
  • Legal compliance of the business with relevant environmental and other regulations

Representing the Seller in Mergers and Acquisitions

When our client in a merger or acquisition is the seller, the primary concern is to protect the seller from any further legal liability after the sale. This is also known as indemnification and it is a highly negotiated aspect of the sale of any business.

We also structure covenants not to compete on the buyer's behalf and draft consulting agreements that allow the seller to stay with the business in a consultant's role for a period of time.

Licensing and Distribution Agreements

We negotiate licensing and distribution contracts on behalf of owners of technology and other marketable products, with the objective of protecting the owner's rights while opening the path for profit. Our clients include computer software companies, oil and gas technology companies and manufacturing companies. We draft licensing and distribution agreements or review existing contracts for potential weaknesses, to make sure that the owner's proprietary information is adequately protected.

Legal Representation of Franchisees

Our law firm also represents individuals or investment groups who wish to purchase a franchise business operation - whether it is a restaurant, a retail outlet or some other consumer-oriented business.

The creation of a franchise distribution system is regulated by both state and federal law, and we have the expertise to counsel a franchisee in this complex area of law.
